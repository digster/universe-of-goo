07 · Balloon lift

The balloon ball adds a per-particle upward force that pulls its attached structure skyward. It's the first place we step outside the uniform gravity field and into per-entity behaviour.

Per-particle force accumulators

Each Particle carries fx, fy force accumulator fields, reset every step. Before the integrator runs, each goo type's applyPreStepBehaviour hook may add its own contribution. For the balloon: 

// src/game/gooball.js
if type === 'balloon':
    scale = attached ? 1 : 0.5        // floats up faster when loaded
    particle.fy -= liftStrength · scale

The Verlet integrator then folds (fx, fy) into the acceleration along with global gravity: 

ax = gx + fx · invMass
ay = gy + fy · invMass
(x, y) += (x - oldX, y - oldY) · (1 - damping) + (ax, ay) · dt²

No special-casing in the integrator — the balloon is just a particle with a non-zero fy. Any gameplay behaviour that looks like "something pulls me" can be expressed the same way: wind, magnets, fans, conveyor belts.

Why the "attached" multiplier?

A balloon that's already floating away from its tower contributes nothing to lifting the structure — so we don't need to pay the full force. Halving the lift when detached makes the scene feel right: free balloons drift up gently; attached ones do the work.

Live demo

The chain is anchored to the floor and rises up to a single balloon ball on top. Toggle lift off to see gravity win (the balloon's weight drags the chain a little); toggle on to watch the whole chain swing upward. The readout shows the balloon's height above the floor.

In the game, levels 03-balloon-lift is built around this mechanic: attach enough balloons to your goo tower and it literally floats the structure across a chasm.

Where to go from here

You've now seen every layer of the engine — integration, constraints, collisions, goo types, attachment rules, per-particle forces. Dive into the game itself and try to build a tower that reaches the goal, or open the sandbox and spawn a hundred balls just to see what happens.
