01 · Particle & Verlet

Everything in this engine is built from one idea: a particle that remembers where it just was. That single fact is enough to integrate gravity, collide off walls, and — in later pages — hold an entire structure together.

Why Verlet, not Euler?

Textbook explicit-Euler integration tracks position and velocity as two separate fields and steps them with v += a·dt; x += v·dt. That works, but position-based constraint solvers (the whole engine from the next page onward) want to edit positions directly. If you move a particle inside the solver, a separately-stored velocity is now out of sync — you'd have to patch it back every time.

Verlet integration sidesteps that by not storing velocity at all. Each particle remembers its current position (x, y) and its position from the previous step (oldX, oldY). The difference between the two is the velocity, implicitly. Move a particle's x and its effective velocity changes automatically — exactly what the constraint solver needs.

The integration step

One step of Verlet on a particle looks like this (src/physics/integrator.js): 

temp   = (x, y)
(x, y) = (x, y) + (x - oldX, y - oldY) · (1 - damping) + a · dt²
(oldX, oldY) = temp

Reading right-to-left: the new position is the old position plus the implicit velocity (scaled down by damping) plus the acceleration term. Then we shuffle the old position forward so next step's (x - oldX) gives us the updated velocity.

Pinned particles set invMass = 0. The integrator skips them and the constraint solver divides their correction by zero weight, so they never move — no branching needed downstream.

Live demo

Drag the damping slider to see how under-damped motion keeps energy, while initial VX gives the particle sideways momentum on launch. Click anywhere on the canvas to relaunch the particle from that spot.

Where this lives in the code

  • src/physics/particle.js — makeParticle(x, y, opts) factory with x, y, oldX, oldY, invMass, radius.
  • src/physics/integrator.js — verletStep(p, dt, gx, gy, damping) is exactly the four lines above.
